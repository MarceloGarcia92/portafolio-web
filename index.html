From business metric to production system

My foundation is Industrial Civil Engineering plus a Master's in Management Control: I first learned ROI and business judgment, then I delved into technical execution. — data scientist, data engineer, data and AI architect. That path gave me something uncommon: I design the architecture and write production-ready code, ensuring the solution is robust, cost-effective, and scale-ready for real users.

I don't turn every problem into an LLM. I use traditional ML when it's the right tool and scale autonomy (agents) only when a simpler design proves insufficient. Every recommendation comes with its trade-off and its cost consideration.

Today I bridge strategic vision with technical execution ITMeet and move toward physical AI and digital twins: perception and agent orchestration as the bridge between data and action in the real world — with a line of doctoral research that formalizes that crossover in agriculture (Agro 5.0).

Doctoral research

Research line

2026 Proposal · PhD in Artificial Intelligence · CRUCh Biobío-Ñuble Consortium
A methodology for autonomous and traceable management control through an agentic architecture, validated in agriculture (Agro 5.0).

Management systems —and Agro 4.0 ones— measure, predict and recommend, but the decision and action over the process still depend on an operator and are rarely traced. I propose an agentic execution layer that closes the strategy → indicators → decision → action loop autonomously and traceably, with explicit capability contracts, extending a management-control framework (Kaplan & Norton's BSC, MAD) with multi-agent architecture.

  • Methodology: Design Science Research (DSR)
  • Validation: instrumented greenhouses in Biobío
  • Data: field IoT sensing + satellite remote sensing
  • Question: does the agentic layer improve the traceability and resilience of decisions vs. a data-science pipeline?
